Tribute to Albert Einstein

Introduction

Albert Einstein was a theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. He was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg, in the German Empire. He died on April 18, 1955, at the age of 76.

Early Life

Einstein was born into a Jewish family in Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg, in the German Empire. His father, Hermann Einstein, was a salesman and engineer. His mother, Pauline Einstein, was a housewife. Einstein had one sister, Maja.

Education

Einstein studied at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich, Switzerland. He graduated in 1900 with a diploma in mathematics and physics. He then worked as a patent clerk in Bern, Switzerland, from 1902 to 1909.

Achievements

  • Developed the theory of relativity
  • Proposed the equation E=mc²
  • Won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921

Legacy

Einstein's work continues to have a major impact on modern physics. His theory of relativity has been confirmed by numerous experiments, and his equation E=mc² has been used to develop nuclear power and nuclear weapons. He is widely regarded as one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century.
